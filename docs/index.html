About me.

I am currently a final year PhD student at Cardiff University where I am supervised by Dr Timothy Davis and Dr Ilaria Ruffa. I am studying nearby AGN to understand how they interact with their host galaxies. I utlise data from telescopes such as ALMA to study how the host galaxy can fuel AGN and how the AGN can impact the host galaxy. I additionally study the emission from AGN to understand the unerlying emission mechanism.

Here is what I am working on

I am currently working on a number of projects studying nearby AGN.

Cicumnuclear AGN Fuelling

I have utlised high resolution CO data from the WISDOM project to study how the molecular gas in galaxies can fuel AGN and how AGN in turn can impact the molecular gas in the host galaxies.

Dense gas around AGN

Utlising both MUSE and ALMA data from the CARS survey I have studied dense gas around nearby AGN to compare the ionisation mechanisms in both the submillimetre and the optical.

Emission from AGN accretion discs

I have studied correlations between the millimetre and X-ray emission to try and understand the underlying accretion and emission mechanims in AGN.
